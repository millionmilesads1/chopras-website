## 17. Dish Page (Restaurant)

**Purpose**: Rank for "[Dish Name] [City]" keywords. Convert dish-level searches into
table reservations or online orders.

**Ideal Word Count**: 700 to 1200 words

**Structure**
```
H1: [Dish Name] in [City] at [Restaurant Name]
Intro paragraph (80 to 120 words):
  - Primary keyword in first sentence
  - The specific angle that makes this restaurant's version of the dish different
  - One verifiable sensory detail (cooking method, temperature, ingredient sourcing)
  - Do NOT open with "If you are looking for..." or "Welcome to..."

H2: What Makes [Dish Name] at [Restaurant Name] Different
  Body: 150 to 200 words
  - The specific technique, ingredient, or preparation that differentiates this dish
  - At least one concrete, verifiable claim (clay oven temperature, sourcing, method)
  - Feature to Benefit to Outcome formula applied to the dish
  - One sensory detail per paragraph — texture, aroma, colour, temperature

H2: The Ingredients Behind [Dish Name]
  Body: 100 to 150 words
  - What goes into this dish at this specific restaurant
  - Any sourcing details that are verifiable and specific
  - Halal status if applicable
  - Vegetarian / vegan status if applicable

H2: How to Order [Dish Name] at [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 80 to 120 words
  - Dine in instructions with link to reservation
  - Online order or takeaway instructions with link
  - Suggested pairings from the menu (link to those dish pages)

H2: Frequently Asked Questions About [Dish Name] in [City]
  H3: Is [dish name] at [restaurant name] halal?
  H3: Can I order [dish name] for delivery or takeaway?
  H3: What makes [restaurant name] [dish name] different from other restaurants?
  H3: Is [dish name] suitable for vegetarians / vegans?
  [Each answer: 50 to 80 words. Direct. Verifiable.]

CTA:
  [Primary: Reserve a table]
  [Secondary: Order online]
  [Micro-commitment: "Open Tuesday to Sunday. Free parking at Leyweg 986."]
```

**Dish Page SEO Rules**
- Primary keyword ([Dish] [City]) must appear in H1, first 100 words, at least one H2,
  meta description, and closing paragraph
- Restaurant name must appear in H1, at least two H2s, and the CTA
- City name must appear minimum 4 times across the page
- Never write generic dish descriptions that any restaurant could use
- Every claim about the dish must be specific to THIS restaurant — not Indian restaurants
  in general
- One FAQ must address a search query format: "Is there [dish] in [city]?" or
  "Where can I find [dish] in [city]?"
- Schema: Service + FAQPage. Include dish name, restaurant, price if known.

## 18. Event and Venue Page (Restaurant)

**Purpose**: Rank for event venue keywords ("[Feestzaal/Venue] [City]") and convert
event inquiries into confirmed bookings. The highest-revenue page type for a restaurant.

**Ideal Word Count**: 1000 to 1500 words

**Structure**
```
H1: [Venue Type] in [City] — [Restaurant Name]
Subheadline: [One sentence: Who the venue serves + the single biggest benefit]

ABOVE THE FOLD:
  - Capacity stated immediately (minimum and maximum guests)
  - Catering included yes or no — stated in the first paragraph
  - Primary CTA: "Request a Free Quote"

H2: What [Restaurant Name] [Venue] Includes
  Body: 150 to 200 words
  - List in benefit format what the venue provides
  - Catering: which cuisines, halal status, menu flexibility
  - Space: capacity, layout options, accessibility
  - Service: what the team handles vs what the client handles

H2: Events We Host at [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 120 to 160 words
  - List every event type with one sentence description each
  - Weddings / nikah receptions, birthdays, corporate dinners, Diwali celebrations,
    anniversary dinners, private parties — whatever applies
  - For each event type: link to the dedicated catering page where one exists

H2: How to Book [Restaurant Name] [Venue]
  Body: 80 to 120 words
  - 3 to 4 step process (simple, reassuring)
  - Step 1: Request a quote. Step 2: Discuss your event. Step 3: Confirm and deposit.
    Step 4: Arrive and enjoy.
  - Emphasise they handle everything

H2: [Location Name] — Getting to [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 60 to 80 words
  - Address with full postcode
  - Parking details
  - Public transport if relevant

H2: Frequently Asked Questions About [Venue Type] in [City]
  H3: How many guests can the [venue] accommodate?
  H3: Is catering included when I hire the [venue]?
  H3: Is the catering halal?
  H3: Can I bring my own decorations?
  H3: What is the pricing for hiring the [venue]?
  [Each answer: 50 to 80 words. Direct. No vague answers.]

CTA (final):
  [Primary: Request a Free Quote]
  [Urgency if real: "We have limited dates available in [month]." Only use if true.]
  [Micro-commitment: "No obligation. Response within 24 hours."]
```

**Event Page SEO Rules**
- Primary venue keyword must appear in H1, first paragraph, at least two H2s,
  meta description, and closing CTA area
- Capacity must be stated clearly — Google looks for this for event venue rich results
- Schema: EventVenue or Service schema + FAQPage
- Every FAQ answer should be a complete standalone response for AI citation
- If the page is in Dutch, write entirely in Dutch — do not mix languages
- Never claim capacity above what the venue actually holds
- CTA must be a quote request, not "contact us" — specific CTAs convert better

## 19. Location Proximity Page (Restaurant)

**Purpose**: Rank for "[Restaurant Type] near [Landmark/City]" or
"[Restaurant Type] [Nearby City]" keywords. Capture searchers in adjacent areas.

**Ideal Word Count**: 600 to 1000 words

**Structure**
```
H1: [Restaurant Type] near [Location] — [Restaurant Name] [Primary City]
Intro paragraph (80 to 120 words):
  - Primary keyword in first sentence
  - Distance or travel time stated immediately and specifically
  - One reason the short journey is worth it (the proof anchor — rating, spices, exclusivity)
  - Do NOT say "Are you looking for" — state the answer directly

H2: How to Get from [Nearby Location] to [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 80 to 120 words
  - Specific route (road number, tram line, or both)
  - Travel time stated with confidence
  - Parking details at the destination
  - This section exists for both the reader and Google Maps proximity signals

H2: Why [Location] Residents Choose [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 100 to 150 words
  - What makes the journey worth it
  - Specific proof: rating, review count, dish specifics, halal certification, exclusivity
  - Community connection where relevant (South Asian community in Rijswijk, etc.)
  - At least one claim that only applies to THIS restaurant

H2: What to Expect When You Visit [Restaurant Name]
  Body: 100 to 150 words
  - Menu highlights most relevant to this audience
  - Halal status if relevant to the likely audience
  - Booking recommendation (busy on weekends, book in advance)
  - Parking and access

H2: Frequently Asked Questions About [Restaurant Type] near [Location]
  H3: Is [Restaurant Name] the closest Indian restaurant to [Location]?
  H3: Can I make a reservation for a group from [Location]?
  H3: Is there halal Indian food near [Location]?
  H3: How far is [Restaurant Name] from [Location]?
  [Each answer: 40 to 70 words. Direct. Specific distances or times.]

CTA:
  [Primary: Reserve your table at [Restaurant Name]]
  [Secondary: Get directions from [Location]]
  [Address + hours clearly stated]
```

**Location Proximity Page SEO Rules**
- Primary location keyword ([Restaurant type] [Nearby city]) in H1, first sentence,
  at least one H2, and meta description
- The main city (Den Haag) appears throughout — this is a local SEO page for the main
  restaurant, not a separate branch
- Distance or travel time must be specific and accurate — never approximate vaguely
- City name of the nearby location appears minimum 4 times on the page
- Schema: LocalBusiness with serviceArea covering the nearby location
- FAQPage schema always included — proximity questions are high People Also Ask traffic
- Internal link to the main homepage and the contact/reservation page mandatory
- Never fabricate landmarks, distances, or travel times — these are verifiable facts
  and inaccurate claims destroy trust immediately
